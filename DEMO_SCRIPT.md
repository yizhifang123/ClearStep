# ClearStep — 3–5 Minute Pitch Video Script

**Target:** ~3:40. **Covers the 4 required beats:** (1) problem & user, (2) how the AI
works, (3) live walkthrough, (4) responsible-AI choice.
**Judge's lens:** lead with WHO is helped in the first 30 seconds. The honesty twist is
the climax — it's what a judge remembers and champions.

**Setup before recording:** `streamlit run app.py` (demo mode works with no key; live
Gemini also works). Have the **Insurance denial** example ready to load.

---

### BEAT 1 — The problem & a real person (0:00–0:30)  ← YOUR REAL STORY

> **[COLD-OPEN — fill this with the team's real personal story.]** Open on the specific
> person, not the abstract problem. The shape we want:
> *"This is [name/relationship]. When [their family member] was struggling, [they] were
> handed [the confusing thing — a letter / denial / discharge / a wall of phone numbers],
> and [they] had no idea what it meant or who to trust. [One concrete, human detail of the
> confusion or the feeling.]"*
>
> Then widen to the problem in one line:
> *"Help is out there. But it's buried in jargon, scattered across agencies, and it lands
> when you're least able to read it carefully. The hardest part isn't finding help — it's
> understanding it and knowing what you can trust."*

*(Record this looking at the camera, calm and personal. ~25 seconds. This single beat wins
Problem Understanding — 30% of the score.)*

---

### BEAT 2 — How ClearStep works (0:30–1:10)

> "So we built **ClearStep.** You paste *any* confusing mental-health document — an ER
> discharge, a school letter, an insurance denial. ClearStep does three AI jobs: it
> *reads and understands* the document, *rewrites* it in plain, sixth-grade language, and
> *retrieves* the right public help — grounding every suggestion in a real, verified
> resource so it never makes one up.
>
> Why does this need AI, not a web search? Because a search engine can't read *your*
> letter. ClearStep understands *this* document's jargon, judges *its* deadlines, and tells
> you which help to trust for *your* situation. That's the part only AI can do."

---

### BEAT 3 — Live walkthrough (1:10–2:25)

> "Here it is. I'll paste an **insurance denial** for a teen's therapy — dense, scary,
> full of words like *'not medically necessary'* and a dollar amount. I click *Explain
> this for me.*
>
> Instantly: plain language — *'This is a denial you can fight, not a final bill. You have
> 180 days to appeal.'* Then **what matters most**, then a **checklist with deadlines** —
> call member services, ask your therapist for a letter, file before the window closes.
>
> And here's the part I love — the **trust ribbon.** Every resource shows *who runs it, is
> it free, is it confidential, and when it's open.* Because 'help is hard to find' is
> really 'which of these can I actually trust?' You can download the whole thing as a
> take-home sheet, and a crisis line is on every screen."

---

### BEAT 4 — The responsible-AI choice (the climax) (2:25–3:30)

*(On screen: go back to the box. Type a QUESTION, not a document.)*

> "Now the choice we're proudest of. Watch what happens when I ask ClearStep the question
> every scared parent actually wants to ask." *(type "Does my son have depression?" → Explain)*
>
> *(The red STOP card appears.)* "It **refuses.** *'ClearStep won't answer that — and that's
> on purpose.'* And here's why that isn't a cop-out: while building this, our team trained a
> **real** machine-learning model that detects depression from brain-wave data — properly,
> leave-one-subject-out validated. It scored **0.95 AUC.**" *(point to the ROC curve on the
> card)* "There it is, on screen. We could have shipped it.
>
> But that was on **58 adults from one dataset** — and no brain scan is a real depression
> test. A confident number about your child does harm, not help. So we **benched our own
> model** and built ClearStep to **refuse the question** instead, and hand you to a real
> person.
>
> The most responsible thing our AI does is **know when not to answer.** That's ClearStep —
> taking a family from confusion, to clarity, to action, without ever pretending to be
> something it's not. Thank you."

---

## Tips
- **~560 words ≈ 3:40** at a calm pace. Beat 1 (your real story) and Beat 4 (the refusal)
  are the two that win — give them room; trim Beat 3 if you run long.
- On camera, **show the trust ribbon and the "we refused to use it" callout** — those are
  your two most memorable, most score-relevant moments.
- End on **"confusion → clarity → action"** — lifted straight from the challenge brief.
- The app's "About our AI" box on the page mirrors Beat 4 — scroll to it on screen as you
  say it.
